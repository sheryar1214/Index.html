Gulab Jamun

Description

Gulab Jamun is a classic Pakistani dessert and is made of milk solids which are deep fried and then immersed in a sugar syrup.

Gulab Jamun Ingredients

  • 1 cup full-fat milk powder
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/8 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (You can find ghee at the store)
  • 5 to 6 tablespoons milk, as needed
  • Oil for deep-frying

Sugar Syrup Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1-1/4 cup water
  • 2 green cardamom pods
  • 1/8 teaspoon rose essence (If using rose water, use 1 to 1-1/2 teaspoons.)
  • 1/4 teaspoon lemon juice (This prevents the sugar syrup from crystallizing.)
Directions
  1. Prepare the dough. In a mixing bowl, add the milk powder, all-purpose flour, baking powder and ghee.
    2. Slowly add the milk, 1 tablespoon at a time, and gently form the dough by mixing.
  2. It should be a stiff dough. If the dough is sticky, grease your palms with oil and gently mix. Do not knead the dough—it will make the jamuns hard.
  3. Let the dough rest. Cover the dough with a damp cloth. Let rest for 5 minutes.
  4. Prepare the sugar syrup, In a saucepan, add the sugar, water and two green cardamom pods, crushed.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil and let the sugar dissolve. When the sugar is completely dissolved, simmer over medium-low heat for 5 to 6 minutes.
    6. We need a sticky-consistency sugar syrup here.
  6. When it’s ready, remove the syrup from the heat and add the rose essence or rose water. Set aside.
  7. Roll the jamuns. Grease your palms and pinch a small amount of dough. Roll gently to form a small, smooth small ball.
  8. Fry the jamuns. In a heavy saucepan, heat a few inches of oil over low heat.The oil should not be very hot while frying the gulab jamuns.
  9. Slowly drop in 4 to 5 balls, depending on the pan size, and deep-fry the jamuns until they turn golden brown.
  10. Using a slotted spoon, keep rotating them in the oil for even cooking and coloring. It takes me 4 to 5 minutes for each batch.
  11. When the jamuns are golden brown, use a slotted spoon to remove them from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  12. Add jamuns to the sugar syrup. Let each jamun sit for 45 to 60 seconds, and then add the fried jamuns to the warm sugar syrup.
    13. Let the jamuns soak in the sugar syrup for at least 2 hours.
  13. Garnish with almond slices and serve! There you have it, heaven on earth!!!!!
